## Unique Positioning
We are the only fragrance house that combines traditional French perfumery techniques with 100% natural, ethically-sourced ingredients, packaged in hand-blown glass bottles.

- "Luxury" (overused, prefer "premium" or "refined")

- "Disrupt" (unless actually disrupting)

- "Base notes" - Long-lasting foundation scents

## Preferred Phrasing
- Instead of "Our products are high quality" ‚Üí "Each piece is crafted with meticulous attention to detail"
- Instead of "We offer" ‚Üí "We create" or "We craft"
- Instead of "You can buy" ‚Üí "Discover" or "Experience"

### **5. Structural Guidelines** ‚≠ê REQUIRED

**What Madison Extracts:**

**Template:**
```
# Structural Guidelines

## Sentence Structure
[Description of preferred sentence structure]
- [Guideline 1]
- [Guideline 2]

## Paragraph Length
[Preferred paragraph length and when to vary]

## Punctuation Style
[How to use punctuation]
- Em-dashes: [When to use]
- Ellipses: [When to use]
- Commas: [Style preference]

## Rhythm Patterns
[How to create rhythm in writing]
- [Pattern 1]
- [Pattern 2]
```

**Example:**
```
# Structural Guidelines

## Sentence Structure
- Mix short, impactful sentences (5-10 words) with longer, descriptive ones (15-25 words)
- Vary sentence length to create rhythm
- Start paragraphs with strong, active verbs when possible
- Use parallel structure for lists

## Paragraph Length
- Keep paragraphs to 2-4 sentences for readability
- Use single-sentence paragraphs sparingly, for emphasis
- Longer paragraphs (5-6 sentences) are acceptable for storytelling sections

## Punctuation Style
- Em-dashes: Use for emphasis, asides, or to create pause
- Ellipses: Use sparingly, only for genuine trailing thoughts
- Commas: Use Oxford commas for clarity
- Semicolons: Use to connect related independent clauses

## Rhythm Patterns
- Start fast, slow down for details, speed up for calls-to-action
- Use alliteration sparingly and naturally
- Create contrast: short sentence after long, simple after complex
```

## üìù Document Formatting Tips

### **Best Practices:**
1. **Use Clear Headings** - Help Madison understand document structure

3. **Include Examples** - Show, don't just tell
4. **List Forbidden Phrases** - Explicitly state what NOT to use
5. **Define Terms** - Explain industry-specific or brand-specific terms
6. **Use Consistent Formatting** - Makes extraction easier

### **What to Avoid:**
- ‚ùå Vague descriptions ("write well")
- ‚ùå Contradictory guidelines
- ‚ùå Missing examples
- ‚ùå Generic marketing speak
- ‚ùå Unclear structure
